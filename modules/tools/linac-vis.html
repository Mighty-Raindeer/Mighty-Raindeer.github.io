Component Information

Electron Gun

Generates the initial electron beam through thermionic emission. Electrons are accelerated by an electric field to form the initial beam.

RF Power Generator

Produces high-power microwave radiation (typically using a magnetron or klystron) that accelerates the electrons through the waveguide.

Accelerator

Accelerating waveguide structure where electrons gain energy from the RF waves through a series of carefully designed cavities.

Electron Beam Transport

System of bending magnets that steer the electron beam through approximately 270Â° to direct it toward the target. Also helps with energy selection.

X-ray Target

High-Z material (typically tungsten) where electrons strike to produce bremsstrahlung X-rays for photon therapy. Includes beam shaping components like flattening filters.

Gantry

Rotatable housing containing the beam transport system and treatment head. Allows beam delivery from any angle around the patient.

Isocenter

The point in space around which the gantry rotates. Treatment is typically planned with the target at isocenter.

Treatment Couch

Precision-engineered patient support system with multiple degrees of freedom for accurate patient positioning.

Stand

Main support structure that houses the RF generator and provides stability for the gantry system.
